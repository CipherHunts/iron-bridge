Part 2: The Code in the Book
The letter ق is one of the mysterious Muqatta'at—the disconnected letters that open 29 chapters of the Quran. No one knows definitively what they mean. The Quran itself calls them "the keys to the inner treasury."

ق appears as an opening letter in two surahs:

Surah 50 (Qaf), where it stands alone: "Qaf. By the Glorious Quran."

Surah 42 (Ash-Shura), where it is part of a five-letter set: حم عسق (Ha Mim, 'Ayn Seen Qaf).

Here, the investigation took its first concrete turn. By counting every occurrence of ق in these two chapters—a count anyone with an Arabic Quran and patience can verify—I found:

In Surah 50: the letter ق appears 57 times.

In Surah 42: the letter ق appears 57 times.

The sum is 114, the total number of chapters in the Quran. 57 = 19 × 3, and 114 = 19 × 6.

This led to a deeper question: Why 57? What does 57 point to?

The 57th chapter of the Quran is Surah Al-Hadid—The Iron. Its verse 25 states: "And We sent down iron, wherein is great might and benefits for mankind." The Arabic word for iron, hadid (حديد), has an Abjad numerical value of 26—the atomic number of iron. Moreover, the stable isotope Iron-57 has a mass number of exactly 57 nucleons.

The letter count of ق, the chapter number of Iron, and an iron isotope's mass number all converge on 57. The symbol from my dream points to the element that modern astrophysics identifies as the cosmic bridge: iron is forged in the cores of massive stars and scattered by supernovae. It becomes the central atom in hemoglobin, the cofactor for dopamine synthesis, the backbone of myelination, and the engine of retinal phototransduction. Iron literally enables the human to perceive and to question. The dream's "iron-sharp sight" (Surah Qaf 50:22) is biologically literal.
